Dr. David Ródenas i Picó

I'm PhD in Computer Architecture specialised in algorithm optimization and creation of tools to ease complex hardware programming. I was born in Barcelona in 1978 and raised in Montgat, a small town near Barcelona. I started to write programs when I was 8 years old, with a ZX Spectrum and a book called "Basic para niños". Before the university I wrote some complex programs, including a simple neuronal net, or a 3D raytracer. The university was for me like an *all you can eat*, full of rich concepts and challenges. When I had my degree, with the opportunity to start a new road for multi-core processors (there was none in the mainstream market) I started to work as intern in the CIRI (CEPBA IBM Research Institute). This is how I began my PhD. When I finished it, I had programmed many kinds of processor architectures, and even designed a generic simulator and my own programming model. Last years I get involved in the startups world, beginning developing the core of a graph database, and currently building data analysis backends to get real-time queries around social recommendation engines.
